GRADIENT

A neural network that trains entirely on your GPU, in the browser — no Python, no server, no WASM. Every kernel — matmul, the backward pass, the optimizer step — is hand-written in WebGPU / WGSL. It trains a 2-layer MLP to ~97% on MNIST and a character-level nano-GPT transformer, with a live loss dashboard, a PCA embedding projector, and a draw-a-digit inference canvas. 44/44 correctness checks validate every kernel against a CPU oracle.

GlassBox

A mechanistic-interpretability lab for GPT-2 small — making a transformer's internals legible from the browser. It renders per-layer/head attention, runs activation patching to causally trace where a fact lives, fits per-layer linear probes to see when a concept becomes decodable, plots the residual stream's PCA trajectory, and surfaces maximally-activating neurons — e.g. a clean "they/their" pronoun detector at L0·N1133.

RAG hallucination attribution

A summer research track at PESU Intelligence Labs on a fundamental gap in RAG systems — today's detection methods tell you an error occurred, but not which retrieved chunk introduced it, which was merely misleading, and which was never useful at all.

The aim: a principled attribution layer that separates systems which fail silently from ones that can be trusted, debugged, and improved in high-stakes deployments.
