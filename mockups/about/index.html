Not A
Card Grid

Seven ways to do the about page as print, not a wall of cards — each in the live neo-brutalist language (paper, ink, red, with lime reserved for position). Same dossier every time: local-first software, black-box ML turned to glass, four shipped projects, and one deadpan cat. Editorial register, columns by construction. Each is a single self-contained HTML file — pick a treatment.

01 OPEN →
The Broadsheet

A newspaper front page. Masthead, the thesis as the lead headline, the bio set in real multi-column body, the cat as the front-page photo with a deadpan caption, a By The Numbers sidebar, and classifieds for contact. Editorial register sits maximally far from a card grid, and columns kill the void by construction. The visual showstopper.

Annotated Essay

One genuinely well-written personal essay, with the right margin turned into a live annotation rail — citations, the cat's dry asides, and ▸ pull-tabs that expand the evidence. The usually-dead right column becomes the feature. Most human, most writerly — a clean contrast to anything visual.

Datasheet

A component datasheet for a person. Part No. PES1UG24CS127 — features, absolute maximum ratings, a real pinout, electrical characteristics, typical applications, and a characteristic curve. Tables and mono, dry and funny, systems-flavored — dense technical documentation reads nothing like a glossy showcase.

Annotated Specimen
