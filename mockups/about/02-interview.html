Q. Local-first software feels almost contrarian right now. Everything is moving to the cloud. Why the conviction?
S.C.

Because your data, your GPU, no server — that's the honest version of computing. With Gradient I trained a 2-layer MLP entirely in the browser, hitting ~97% MNIST accuracy using raw WGSL compute shaders on your own GPU. No backend, zero dependencies. 44/44 kernel checks pass against an f64 CPU oracle. The model literally never leaves your machine.


Orbit — my focus engine — does the same thing for productivity: sub-5ms scheduling, IndexedDB with snapshot recovery, works offline, ships as a PWA. The internet is optional. And I think that's the direction things should go more often, especially for ML tooling. We've got GPUs sitting idle in every laptop while we pipe data to distant servers. [long pause] That seems like a solved problem we're choosing not to solve.
