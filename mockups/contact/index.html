The Callback

LET'S TALK actually places the call. Click it and the screen dials out — DIALING SANTOSH…, rings, connects — and lands on a brutalist voicemail composer that sends for real (mailto, or Formspree for a live inbox). The socials become magnetic speed-dial discs that lean toward the cursor, fill-invert, ring-pulse and spin like the Saturn monogram. The email decodes in and clicking it punches a hard COPIED ✓ with a clack and a red invert. A time-aware line reads your live clock, the empty red grid lights up under the cursor, a guestbook lets visitors stamp the wall, and a sleepy pixel cat dozes in the corner — z z z — until you reach for LET'S TALK, then perks up. It ends on a stamped EOF and one last Saturn spin.

Slide to Send

Blow up the headline and make the arrow do something. LET'S TALK fills the band — TALK in a huge hollow outline that inks in as you slide. The → is a physical slide-to-unlock: grab it, shove it across the width, and at the wall it fires the call — slide-to-unlock energy, hard and mechanical, with ticking detents. Release short and it snaps back with a thunk. Keyboard and tap fallbacks fire the same dial-out → voicemail flow. The slider is "call me direct"; the empty right column becomes a speed-dial rack — numbered 01–04 like the Experience switchboard, with GitHub + LinkedIn pinned on top (★, live ● dot, GitHub pulling a real repo count from the public API), Email (tap-to-copy) and Résumé beneath. Icons spin like the Saturn monogram, labels decode-in. Same world — reactive grid, live clock, sleeping cat, stamped EOF and a final Saturn spin — but the gesture is the signature nobody forgets.
